28.5 million people or about 11,2% of total population in Indonesia live bellow the poverty line. (BPS, 2015)
It would lead to:
Unhealthy sanitation
Lack of health access
Poverty forces people to live on informal lands. As they occupy slums, their basic sanitation, and health, are at risk. Poor public health service means that people would need to rely on private health service and one would need to be of a certain socio-economic status to afford that.
There are 3,201 identified slums in Indonesia with total area 34, 374 hectare. (Ministry of Public Work, 2014) There are only 5.6 hospitals per 100,000 people in Indonesia. (World Bank, 2008).
